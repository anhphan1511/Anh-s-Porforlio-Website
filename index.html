The Impact of COVID-19 Mortality on Labor Participation

Conducted an empirical study across 61 developed and developing countries to examine how COVID-19 death rates affected labor force participation in 2021. Built multiple OLS regression models using variables such as mortality, GDP per capita, vaccination rates, government response, and education levels. Found a statistically significant negative relationship between mortality and labor force participation, with stronger effects in developing countries, highlighting the role of healthcare systems and policy in labor market resilience.
